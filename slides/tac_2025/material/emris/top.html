Extreme mass ratio inspirals

Extreme mass ratio inspirals (EMRIs) represent a prime target for LISA

They will undergo hundreds of thousands of waveform cycles while staying in the LISA band

Unique opurtunity to test fundamental physics and probe the astrophysical environments of MBHs

We need fast, accurate waveforms

Being long lived sources with a large number of cycles, EMRIs require accurate waveform models to have a small phase error: $\Delta \boldsymbol{\Phi} \sim 1 / \rho$.

Speed is crucial to explore their huge parameter space

Two approaches:

Gravitational self-force (GSF)

Kludge models

the state of the art: Fastemriwaveforms

Fastemriwaveforms (Katz:2021yft) is public tool that provides EMRI waveforms on (hundreds of) milliseconds timescales.

Two approaches:

(Post-) adiabatic waveforms in Schwarzschild spacetimes

Kludge waveforms with a 5PN informed trajectory in generic geometries

Goal for LISA: Post adiabatic waveforms in generic Kerr spacetimes
